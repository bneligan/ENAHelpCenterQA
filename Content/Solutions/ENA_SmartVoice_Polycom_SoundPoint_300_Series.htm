What is a soft key?

Watch video

Soft keys appear at the bottom of your screen and change depending on where you are in a call sequence. You select a soft key by pressing the physical button directly underneath it.

One of the most helpful tips to using your Polycom SoundPoint 300 series phone is to watch the soft keys. The system is very good about providing you with a soft key for whatever you are most likely to do next in a call sequence.

What is my extension?

Your personal extension shows shows on the screen.

Why do I have two keys for my extension?

Having two keys makes it much easier to answer a second incoming call or place a caller on hold and make a second outbound call.

Watch the video

Press Answer or the Line Key that is flashing green.

Make Calls

You do not have to dial 9 or any other prefix to make an outbound call.

You can dial any other ENA SmartVoice phone in your system using just the phone’s 4 or 5 digit extension.

How do I make a call using the handset?

  1. Dial the number on the keypad.
  2. Pick up the handset to start the call.

Be aware if you pick up the handset before starting to dial, the system attempts to complete the call after an approximately 2 second pause. You will get an error if you have not entered all the digits. To avoid this, leave the handset in the cradle while dialing and pick up when finished.

How do I make a call using the speakerphone?

  1. Dial the number on the keypad.
  2. Press Dial or press the speakerphone button.

Hold Calls

How do I place a caller on hold?

When you are on an active call, press the Hold soft key.

How do I take a caller off hold?

When a caller is on hold, the line key will blink red. You may press that line key or press the Resume soft key to return to the caller.

If you have two callers on hold, or are on an active call and have one caller on hold, use the navigation disk to highlight the call to which you would like to return, then press Resume.

Transfer Calls

Consultative (Announced) transfer

Watch the video

Sometimes, you want to talk to the transfer recipient before sending the caller through. For example, you may want to let the recipient know who the caller is, give the recipient some background information, check and see if the recipient is available to take the call, etc.

In these cases, you use a Consultative transfer.

  1. Press the Trnsfer soft key.
  2. Dial the recipient.
  3. After you have spoken with the recipient and are ready to complete the transfer, press the Trnsfer button again.

When you press Transfer, the original caller is automatically placed on hold. You do not have to manually place the caller on hold.

Blind transfer

Watch the video

To transfer a call to a recipient without talking with the recipient first, you use the BlindXfer soft key.

  1. Press Blindxfer.
  2. Dial the recipient’s extension or number.
  3. Press Enter.

To transfer a call to a recipient without talking with the recipient first if you do not see a Blindxfer soft key:

  1. Press Trnsfer.
  2. Press Blind.
  3. Dial the recipient.
  4. Press Send.

Direct to voicemail transfer

Watch the video

To transfer a caller directly to someone’s voicemail if do not see a VMxfer soft key and have a 4 digit extension:

  1. Press Transfr.
  2. Press Blind.
  3. Dial *90 followed by the recipient's number.
  4. Press Send.

To transfer a caller directly to someone’s voicemail if have a 5-6 digit extension:

  1. Press Transfer.
  2. Press Blind.
  3. Dial * followed by the recipient's number.
  4. Press Send.

Cancel a transfer

Press the Cancel soft key at any point prior to completing a transfer to return to the original call.

You may have to press More to see the Cancel soft key.

Recent Calls List

Watch video

When your phone is in the Lines view (where you see your extension number next to keys), the arrows on your phone’s navigation dial are shortcuts to your recent calls lists.

Down arrow = Received

Right arrow = Placed

To place a call from your Recent Calls lists:

  1. Press the arrow on the navigation dial to open the list.
  2. Use the up and down arrows on the navigation dial to highlight the number you would like to call.
  3. Press Dial.

Make a Conference Call

Watch video

When you are on a call with one party:

  1. Press Conf soft key.
  2. Dial the third party.
  3. When the third party answers, press Conference again.

To add a fourth person, repeat the process.

Use Do Not Disturb (DND)

Do Not Disturb (DND) sends all incoming calls directly to voicemail.

Do Not Disturb (DND) sends all incoming calls directly to voicemail. You toggle Do Not Disturb on and off by pressing:

  1. Menu
  2. Features
  3. Do Not Disturb

If you are not receiving calls, check to make sure Do Not Disturb is not activated on your phone.

Park Calls

Call Park allows you to place a call on hold so it can be retrieved from any other ENA SmartVoice phone in your building.

How do I park a call?

  1. Press Trnsfer.
  2. Dial *98.
  3. You will hear a message telling you on which orbit the call is parked and prompting you to complete the transfer. Press Transfer to complete.

How do I pick up a parked a call?

When you let a person know they have a parked call, you will need to tell them the orbit number on which the call is parked. To pick up a parked call, go to any ENA SmartVoice phone in the building and dial *99 followed by the orbit number.
