Connecting to your network via ENA NetShield VPN

Prior to activating your ENA NetShield VPN, your ENA Project Manager will provide you or your TC with the following:

  • IP address for your VPN connection
  • Group name
  • Shared Secret/Pre-shared key

Connecting on a machine running Mac OS 10.6 or later

Setting Up Your ENA NetShield VPN Connection

  1. Open System Preferences in the Apple menu.

  2. Open Network.

  3. Click the plus sign.

  4. Select VPN from Interface drop down menu.
  5. Select Cisco IPSec from VPN Type drop down menu.
  6. Type in the Service Name of your choice.

  7. Enter the Server Address.
  8. Enter your network Account Name.
  9. Enter your network Password.

  10. Click Authentication Settings.

  11. Enter the Shared Secret.
  12. Enter the Group Name.
  13. Click OK.

Starting an ENA NetShield VPN Session

  1. Open System Preferences in the Apple menu.

  2. Open Network.

  3. Highlight the VPN network and click Connect.

  4. Enter your network Account Name and Password.
  5. Click OK.

  6. If the connection was successful, status will show as Connected.

Connecting on a machine running Windows OS using Shrew Soft

Setting Up Your ENA NetShield VPN Connection

  1. Download the most current version of Shrew Soft from www.shrew.net/download/vpn.

  2. Open the installer.

  3. Click Next.

  4. Select Standard Edition and click Next.

  5. If you accept terms of License Agreement, click I Agree.

  6. Click Next.

  7. Click Next.

  8. Click Finish.

  9. Open the VPN Access Manager.

  10. Click Add.

  11. Under the General tab, type in the Host Name.
  12. Select ike config pull from the Auto Configuration drop down menu.

  13. Under the Authentication tab, select Mutual PSK+XAuth from the Authentication Method drop down menu.

  14. Under Local Identity choose Key Identifier from the Identification Type drop down menu.

  15. Type the Group Name in the Key ID String field.

  16. Under Credentials, enter the Pre-Shared Key.

  17. Under the Phase 2 tab, select group 2 in the PFS Exchange drop down.

  18. Click Save.

Starting an ENA NetShield VPN Session

Open the VPN Access Manager.

Click on network to select.

Click Connect.

Enter your network Username and Password.

Click Connect.

If connection is successful, will see tunnel enabled status.
